In in vivo spike recordings and animal behavior behavior is thought of in epochs of time which is part of the experimental trial design.
During an individual epoch neurons in the cerebellum function in a sequence and not homogeneously, and the sequence is meaningful.
How was that discovered?
-Cerebellar eyelid conditioning is a way of studying the cerebellum unusually directly. There are two inputs to the cerebellum, mossy
and climbing fibers. Thus there are 2 inputs. One input is the tone and the other is the puff of air into the eye. Learning happens in
the cerebellum and the cerebellum drives the expression of the eyelid responses where the animal learns to close its eyelid when the tone 
comes on. When the time lag between the tone and the threat to the eye is varied the cerebellum responds differently depending on the time lag. 
When the time lag is 1.5 seconds apart the cerebellum drives the expression of the eyelid response exactly 1.5 seconds after the next tone 
but if the time lag is 0.25 seconds apart the cerebellum drives the response at exactly the same time the tone is on.This suggests the cerebellum 
is not only encoding that the tone is on but how long it has been on. For a snapshot of the sequence of neuronal activity of the cerebellum at a point 
in time we can infer how long the tone has been on. The popular idea among neuroscientists is that once an input goes to a brain region it takes a layer of neurons to encode the input. That happens woth granule cells in the cerebellum. There has been a new synapse discovered that makes the encoding less sensitive to stray action potentials that the old models failed to predict. The pattern of neuronal activity must be constant for each trial of stimulus otherwise the cerebellum cannot learn to encode for the time the stimulus has been on.
